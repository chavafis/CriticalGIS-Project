## Aim
The aim of this project is to explore and analyze the historical presence and distribution of Jewish communities in Mediterranean cities during the early 20th century, utilizing Geographic Information Systems (GIS) to map and visualize spatial data related to demographic, cultural, and socio-economic aspects.

## Project Description
This Critical GIS project focuses on the Jewish communities in Mediterranean cities between 1890 and 1930. It involves collecting, processing, and analyzing geospatial data to understand the spatial patterns, migrations, and cultural significance of these communities. The project will include maps, data visualizations, and analyses to provide insights into the historical context of Jewish life in the Mediterranean region during this period.

## Top 5 Mediterranean Cities with Major Jewish Communities (1890-1930)
Based on historical records and population data from the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the top 5 Mediterranean cities with major Jewish communities during this period were:

1. **Thessaloniki, Greece** - Home to one of the largest Sephardic Jewish communities in Europe, with approximately 80,000 Jews before World War II.
2. **Istanbul, Turkey** - A significant center for Jewish life, with a diverse community including Sephardic and Ashkenazi Jews.
3. **Alexandria, Egypt** - Known for its vibrant Jewish community, which played a key role in the city's cultural and economic life.
4. **Cairo, Egypt** - Another major hub for Jewish population in Egypt, with a long history of Jewish presence.
5. **Smyrna (Izmir), Turkey** - Featured a substantial Jewish community, particularly Sephardic Jews, contributing to the city's multicultural fabric.
